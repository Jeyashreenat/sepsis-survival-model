# sepsis-survival-model The data used in this study is publicly available in the UC Irvine Machine Learning Repository [5]. The dataset consists of 110,204 admissions of 84,811 hospitalized subjects between 2011 and 2012 in Norway who were diagnosed with infections, systemic inflammatory response syndrome, sepsis by causative microbes, or septic shock. The patient data is accessed via electronic health records and has been studied for retrospective model fit analysis and outcome prediction. Sepsis is associated with immediate death risk, often killing patients within one hour. Hence it is important to develop a statistically strong model for risk categorization and timely delivery of care. Based on the risk category of the patient, hospitals can efficiently triage care and  treatment leading to successful patient outcomes as well as cost saving to the hospitals.
